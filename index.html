The Great Wall of China

Location: China
Date Built: Over several dynasties, with significant construction during the Ming Dynasty (1368 TO 1644).
Details: The Great Wall of China is not a traditional bridge but a series of fortifications built to protect against invasions. Stretching over 13,000 miles, it is one of the most famous architectural wonders, utilizing various materials such as earth, wood, bricks, and stone.

The Pont du Gard, France

Location: Near Nîmes, France
Date Built: 1st century AD (Roman era)
Details: A well-preserved Roman aqueduct bridge, the Pont du Gard spans the Gardon River. It served as part of a sophisticated water supply system for the city of Nemausus (Nîmes). The three-tiered structure stands as a testament to ancient Roman engineering prowess.

Millau Viaduct, France

Location:Millau, France
Date Completed2004
Details:The Millau Viaduct is the tallest bridge in the world, with its highest mast reaching 343 meters (1,125 feet) above the base of the structure. Designed by architect Norman Foster, it spans the Tarn River Valley and is renowned for its aesthetic and engineering excellence.

Golden Gate Bridge, USA

Location:San Francisco, California
Date Completed1937
Details:The Golden Gate Bridge is an iconic suspension bridge known for its distinctive International Orange color. With a main span of 1,280 meters (4,200 feet), it connects San Francisco to Marin County. The Art Deco design and engineering excellence make it one of the most recognized and photographed bridges globally.

Tower Bridge, United Kingdom

Location:London, England
Date Completed 1894
Details:Tower Bridge is a bascule and suspension bridge over the River Thames. Its iconic twin towers and Gothic architectural elements contribute to its global recognition. The bridge's bascules can be raised to allow ships to pass, adding to its functional and aesthetic appeal.

Millau Viaduct, France

Location:Millau, France
Date Completed 2004
Details:The Millau Viaduct is a cable-stayed bridge that stands as the tallest bridge in the world, reaching a height of 343 meters (1,125 feet). Spanning the Tarn River Gorge, it is celebrated for its elegant design and use of high-strength, self-compacting concrete.

Siduhe Bridge, China

Location:Hubei Province, China
Date Completed 2009

Rialto Bridge, Italy

Location:Venice, Italy
Date Built:Completed in 1591
Details:The Rialto Bridge is one of the four bridges spanning the Grand Canal in Venice. Completed in 1591, it is the oldest of the four bridges and a prime example of Renaissance architecture. The bridge's central arch was a significant engineering achievement of its time.

The Charles Bridge, Czech Republic

Location: Prague, Czech Republic
Date Built: (1357 TO 1402)

The Ponte Vecchio, Italy

Location: Florence, Italy
Date Built: Originally constructed in Roman times; current bridge built in 1345.
Details: The Ponte Vecchio is a medieval stone bridge famous for its unique shops, traditionally occupied by jewelers and goldsmiths. Spanning the Arno River, the bridge has survived floods and wars, making it a symbol of Florence's rich history and architectural charm.

Brooklyn Bridge, USA

Location:New York City, New York
Date Completed1883
Details: The Brooklyn Bridge is a hybrid cable-stayed/suspension bridge that connects Manhattan and Brooklyn. It was one of the first steel-wire suspension bridges, and its neo-Gothic towers and web of cables contribute to its iconic appearance. The bridge played a crucial role in the development of New York City.

Pont des Arts, France

Location:Paris, France
Date Completed1804 (original); 1984 (current)
Details:The Pont des Arts, also known as the "Love Lock Bridge," spans the Seine River in Paris. While the original bridge was completed in 1804, the current bridge was reconstructed in 1984. It became famous for the tradition of attaching padlocks (love locks) to its railings, symbolizing love and commitment.

Location:Connects Malmö, Sweden, and Copenhagen, Denmark
Date Completed2000
Details:The Øresund Bridge is a combined railway and motorway bridge that transforms into an underwater tunnel. It connects Sweden and Denmark, providing a vital transportation link. The bridge-tunnel system is a marvel of engineering and international collaboration.
